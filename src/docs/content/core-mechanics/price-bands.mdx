# Price Bands & Outcomes

Every bet is placed on a price band — a fixed interval $[\text{lowerPrice},\, \text{upperPrice})$ for a specific market and 5-second window.

## Band Definition

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `lowerPrice` | Inclusive lower bound |
| `upperPrice` | Exclusive upper bound (`lowerPrice + priceCellSize`) |

Fixed band widths per market:

| Market | Band Width |
|--------|------------|
| BTC/USDT | $25.00 |
| JUP/USDT | Coming Soon |
| SOL/USDT | $0.10 |

The upper bound is exclusive so every price belongs to exactly one band — no ambiguity at boundaries.

---

## Settlement Rule

> The settlement price is the OHLC **close** of the last completed candle whose timestamp is at or before `window_end`.

This rule is enforced identically in two places:
1. **Backend tick evaluator** — writes `WIN` or `LOSS` to the database
2. **ZK circuit** — independently recomputes the same candle close and verifies each order's outcome

If they disagreed, the ZK proof would fail.

**WIN:** $\text{settlement\_close} \in [\text{lowerPrice},\, \text{upperPrice})$

**LOSS:** $\text{settlement\_close} \notin [\text{lowerPrice},\, \text{upperPrice})$

---

## Why Candle Close, Not High/Low

Using a price spike (High/Low) would allow intrabar spoofing: place a bet, execute a brief spike into the band, collect the payout. The close requires sustained buying/selling pressure to move, is agreed upon globally across all participants, and is independently fetchable from OHLC APIs.

The Pyth oracle cross-validates the close at three batch checkpoints (max 2% deviation), making it impossible for the backend to fabricate a settlement price that diverges materially from the on-chain oracle.
