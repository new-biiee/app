# Reward Rate Model

Every cell's reward rate is a function of two normalized variables:

$$\text{rewardRate} = f(dx,\, dy)$$

where:
- $dx = (t_{\text{cell}} - t_{\text{now}}) / \text{gridXSizeMs}$ — normalized time offset
- $dy = |p_{\text{cell}} - p_{\text{now}}| / \text{priceCellSize}$ — normalized price distance

## Model Structure

$f$ is a **polynomial-log parametric model**:
- **Logarithmic terms in $dx$:** cells one step ahead have slightly higher win probability, with the difference growing logarithmically — terms like $\log(dx + m)$ to avoid singularity at $dx = 0$
- **Polynomial terms in $dy$:** the price-distance to win-probability relationship is approximately polynomial over the finite grid range

Coefficients were calibrated offline. The model shape matches the theoretical fair-value surface from Hawkes-driven jump-diffusion simulation.

## Offline Calibration

**Step 1 — Jump-Diffusion Monte Carlo (Sobol QMC)**

For each grid position $(dx, dy)$, simulated price paths under a Hawkes-driven jump-diffusion process:

$$dS_t = \mu S_t \, dt + \sigma_t S_t \, dW_t + J_t \, dN_t(\lambda_t)$$

where $\lambda_t$ is the Hawkes intensity from the volatility engine. Paths were drawn using **Quasi-Monte Carlo Sobol sequences** — low-discrepancy sequences that fill the state space uniformly and give 10–30× variance reduction over pseudo-random sampling for the same path count.

The fraction of paths with $S_T \in [L, U)$ gave empirical win probability $\hat{P}(dx, dy)$.

**Step 2 — Kou Barrier Correction**

For near-barrier cells (small $dy$), applied the Kou jump-diffusion barrier extension:

$$P_{\text{touch}} = e^{-2(x-b)(y-b)/\sigma^2 T} + \Delta_{\text{Kou}}(\lambda,\, p,\, \eta_1,\, \eta_2,\, T)$$

$\Delta_{\text{Kou}}$ accounts for the additional barrier-touch probability from jump arrivals. At negligible jump intensity it collapses to the plain diffusion formula — no discontinuity as $\lambda \to 0$.

**Step 3 — Model Fitting**

Fit $f(dx, dy)$ to minimize MSE between $1/f(dx, dy)$ (implied win probability) and the corrected estimates $\hat{P}(dx, dy)$.

## Platform Edge & Fortress Spread

**Platform edge** (`PLATFORM_EDGE_BPS = 700`, 7%): uniform downward scaling of fair-value multipliers. Instead of $\text{multiplier} = 1/\hat{P}$, the model targets $\approx 1/\hat{P} \times 0.93$. Embedded at commitment time — users can verify the edge by comparing offered multipliers against theoretical $1/\hat{P}$ for any $(dx, dy)$.

**Fortress spread** (`fortressSpreadBps = 150` in NORMAL regime): additional non-uniform tilt based on current VolatilityRegime. Widens to 300 bps (HIGH) or 500 bps (EXTREME). The full production system uses Avellaneda-Stoikov optimal half-spread derived from live $\sigma_t$ and inventory state.

## Signature Commitment

The ZK circuit verifies that each settled order's `rewardRate` matches the `gridSignature`-committed value. The model can't be changed retroactively for a cell already in play.
